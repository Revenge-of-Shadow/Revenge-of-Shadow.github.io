Awakening

made 20241201

I was tasked with a simple if-else-based text adventure.

It was not enough.


I created something akin to a visual novel engine - only displaying text and options as input-output in terminal.


I made it as clean and as thorough as no program I wrote before. It has a lot to do with the fact that C and C++ are my favourite programming languages, but it was also well after I finished studying programming in an academy. I changed my point of view on how to make things and went with "the only way to go fast is to go good", as a close friend briefly mentioned to me.


The typerwriter is displayed for a reason - she played a big role in inspiring me. I found Erika accidentally by a flower shop, asked about her and was suddenly proposed to take her with me. I could not hesitate. Moreover, I whined and cried in fascination.

Until now I love Erika still. This is a marvelous device of a distant era of more elegant technologies.


The stories came to me mostly by themselves. I would sit down in the evening and collect random thoughts together. I did not want to go overboard with making those into fine novels. I wrote them with no second thoughts to fill the game with content to explore.

And I was surprised how well it went.

Grabber

20250419-unfinished

Once I saw an accumulator beyond a fence, just out of my reach. I wanted to find a solution.


I considered different schemes with passions, I calculated the force, estimated the shape and components.

And then I got into three-dimensional modelling.


I discovered FreeCAD, got comfortable with it and became entirely immersed in making meshes. It was just like a game to me, with freedom to create.


I stopped at the moment when I understood that I have to perform tests.

I printed a couple of gears at a HackerSpace from plastic and found that they perform very, very poorly. I read that resin is much more suitable for such purposes. Moreover, I needed to implement shafts, for which I would have needed to get tiny polished metal rods, and, overall, I ended up with the fact that I have no resources to materialize what I shaped.


I enjoyed modelling so much that I started making detailed macros for typical technical parts. This I later separated into "FreeCAD-TechnoMacros".

FreeCAD-TechnoMacros

last major update: 20251109

This has got to be my most practical project of all.


Fascinated with possibilities of modelling, I learned of FreeCAD macros. At first, I needed a model of a spring to see how it fits and extrudes.

I followed tutorials and learned, as was recommended, from the commands that graphical user interface displays during normal operations.

I have got the ability to type in parameters and make springs, then, ball bearings, screws, nuts and gears - all how I want, even hilariously impractical shapes - all at my fingertips!

Actively studying for the semester's hardest subject, I was entirely desperate. I suddenly remembered that mathematical concepts are best explained by 3Blue1Brown.

Phasor-Diagram

made 20251122

On metrology laboratory lecture I was told that I have to include a precise diagram into report.

By the end of that sentence I was too far gone.


Having never used Qt on C++, I rushed at it and completed the program in just one night.


I proudly presented it to the department. It helped some students make their reports with good quality and less effort, still learning something good.

It really struck me when a lad whom I do not know thanked me for the program and pointed out bugs and suggestions.

I was grateful for both the praise and the criticism. I rushed to upgrade the program into a much more usable version.

I also found that my initial angle calculations were made arctan(arctan(a/b)) accidentally. I fixed it quickly but included the wrong results in the report.
This is shameful but amusing.

TalkToHer

last major update: 20251214

One year after Awakening I decided to make it into a truthful visual novel as an overkill for university programming project.


I faced various difficulties, related to object-oriented-programming planning, SFML issues, language version issues and drawing.


This was, by far, my most meticulous project and I am very proud of this.

My favourite part was creating my own scenario script interpreter.


SFML window resizing happened to be entirely broken. I had to write a specific universal fix, having figured out the pattern prior to that.


I proudly used pointers.


I tried to install newer version of C++ because of one function.

My Xubuntu system broke because of that on university morning.

I installed Manjaro immediately afterwards and I am very glad I did so.


I was surprised to find that making character sprites was much more difficult than I imagined.

In fact, sprites are the only thing that the project lacks now other than sounds.


This project has a good potential for updates.


I love C++.

Long-Handles

A Minecraft 1.16.5 modification

20251226

In fact, I decided that I want to learn programming about ten years ago exactly because I wanted to make modifications for Minecraft.


Well...

Although it was a nice little challenge at first, it became a mess.

The problem is that 1.16.5 does not have any documentation. It might have existed, in a way, but now it is replaced with newer versions.


With no documentation to refer to I was forced to use the abomination that is generative artificial intelligence.

It was useful to some extent. Until it started confusing versions and lying me about the methods that do not exist.


This was a very obnoxious experience, lasting for days of constant anger.
