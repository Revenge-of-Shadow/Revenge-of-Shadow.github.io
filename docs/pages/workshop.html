Car

made 20220116; reviewed 20251222 0814


This has got to be my craziest invention.


On the very beginning of high school, already being know as a passionate tinkerer, I found a pile of planks on the way to school and just could not pass by.

It took me a good half a year of hard work to assemble this nonsense, planning on the go. I just screwed all the planks together, segment by segment, all whilst adding crazy ideas like doors opening upwards, fan, bass-busted speakers, heater, key-controlled power supply and other things I no longer remember.

It was absurd. It was huge. It was made about exclusively from junk.

It was very fun.

The teachers were politely impressed, the technologies teacher was just shocked; the class did not give me much credit. I myself can barely remember I created this.

And yet I did and it was fun.

Lightsaber

made 20220618; reviewed 202512220 0840

I was a Star Wars fanatic for years, having read circa one hundred and twenty Star Wars novels before mid-middle school. I still posess great knowledge of the "galaxy far, far away", but at some moment I have fallen out of it.


I wanted a lightsaber of my own. In fact, once I bought a cheap one. And it was full of plastic and just too dim.


I kept thinking on making my own lightsaber model for years. I asked my grandfather to cut me a piece of pipe and he did so, which I am grateful for. It took me a long time to become savvy enough to prepare all the parts and put them together.


And I made it. Couple of transistors, resistor, capacitor, speaker, found accumulator, button switch I repeatedly colored black and forty-two light-emitting diodes. Because fifty did not fit in inside of the tube.


I took the plastic tube from the bought lightsaber and added bubble wrapping inside of it to spread the light smoothly and affix the diodes.


I was considering the color for some time. I naturally wanted it to keep the blue color of my idolized Jedi, Anakin Skywalker; still I decided to go with my favourite color after black at that time. Not because of the Sith. Because red pairs very well with black.

Metal glove

made 20221024; reviewed 20251222 0844

The most impressive of my projects.


Perhaps I was very inspired by Loremaster. I can not recall what was the spark that caused the ignition for my desire to make a metal glove.


In the high school I schemed and assembled a glove out of countless tin cans. All by myself, with no references, no prequisite studies of armor. Nothing but ingenuity, paper, pencils, markers, tin cans, scissors, drill, screw and nuts. I was scheming on lessons and in my free time.


I was sketching, thinking how to connect one part to another.


And it worked.


I am still very proud of it.

Metal menace

made 20230629; reviewed 20251222 0921

'Tis but a tragedy.


I am since long passionate with cybernetics - in my imagination it is nothing but personally controlled mechatronic devices. Huge humanoid mechas I saw in some cartoons and daydreams, robotic creation of Loremaster, and, importantly, drawings with robotic hands directly controlled by inventors.


I had parts of all kinds. I had Arduino boards. Eventually, I found inspiration.

Initially, "Project: Metal menace" and "Project: Metal glove" were one. I divided them for practical reasons.

I tirelessly worked on this dream, seeking parts and solutions, forming elements.


I did not expect my poor mechanics knowledge to backfire this badly.

It was the most basic principle - the lever rule. I did not account for it and servomotors broke after struggling to lift the mechanical arms.


The failure left me in despair for a long time.

Nukeball

made 20230718; reviewed 20251222 1145

I always thought that thermal detonator is cool. In best iterations, it beeps slowly, then faster and faster until the timer goes off and sets a sizeable explosion from just one nice spherical object.


Years of tinkering were not for nothing, as it seemed, since I collected some knowledge having burned eight holes through my jacket with just one shorted accumulator that had a back-up switch.


I decided that making electronical timer is just too difficult and unnecessary. So I calculated how much time it would take for a wire to get enough heat to ignite gunpowder around it. I thereby estimated the time. I tried igniting gunpowder separately with shorted wire and then assembled this device.

Thank code I never tested the theory.

ProtoSentry

made 20240913; reviewed 20251222 1425

My most presentable project.


I got into Team Fortress 2 at some point in my life. Pyro was a close call, but I could not be any more fitting to be Engineer.

Naturally, cosplay was just not enough. What is it worth if someone just dresses as a character? Nay, one must become that persona!


It was hard. It was very hard.

I had to shape metal with what little tools I had at hand. I was cutting it with strong scissors and bending it with a small hammer. I drew schematics. I pondered.


The worst happened when I started with electronics, unsurprisingly. The servomotors were proven to be too weak for full mobility, so I was forced to keep only one axis. Arduino had proven to be too weak to play sounds and rotate motors, so I used two Arduino Nano boards in a horrific mess of wires that barely fit into the metal body. Classic sonic distance sensors SR04 failed repeatedly, so I just soldered two of them in parallel for no reason and let them be.


More than once I wanted to give up on this project. I did not - out of desperation. I can not stand giving up on something that was very close to working.

My girlfriend at the time supported me greatly, which helped a lot. I am still grateful for all she had done for me.


I finished it and brought it to a cosplay convention. On it it got progressively more broken over time, requiring me to screw it back together and tackle with tape. By evening it barely worked.

I fixed it and gave it to my fellow Engineer cosplayer. It broke on the way and was never fixed.

D8

I decided to try making three-dimensional models all by myself. I learned OpenSCAD the hard way, but I managed to make all of the essential Dragons and Dungeons dies... except for D20.

I used the little hospitality that HackerSpace had for me to print it.


Months later I gathered my courage and recalculated D20, finishing it. It was worth it – I am proud of the result.


I never got the ability to print them all - HackerSpace community was too hard for me to withstand and I myself have no fitting printer.

I keep the die in my backpack. On rare occasions it comes to be useful - I once was honored by a lecturer as he took it to choose a student to answer by the chalkboard.
